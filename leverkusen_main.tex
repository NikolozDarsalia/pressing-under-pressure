\section{Overview}\label{overview}

\textbf{Research Question:} Does Bayer Leverkusen's 2023/24 unbeaten
season reflect a distinctive tactical system --- specifically, an
anomalous spatial pressing response under losing conditions --- or is
aggressive late-game pressing common among elite Bundesliga teams?

\textbf{Paper Structure:}

\begin{itemize}
\item
  \textbf{Section 2} --- Data collection: StatsBomb event data + ClubElo
  opponent ratings
\item
  \textbf{Section 3} --- Motivating evidence: Where does Leverkusen
  press, and where do they engineer turnovers? (KDE + zone polygons +
  raster trap rates)
\end{itemize}

\emph{Answer:} Wide attacking channels (A3\_R: 35.9\% counterpress
rate), robust to opponent quality.

\begin{itemize}
\tightlist
\item
  \textbf{Section 4} --- Does Leverkusen systematically shift their
  press forward when losing late? (Difference-in-Differences)
\end{itemize}

\emph{Answer:} Yes, with a +6.17m DiD estimate in the full window (p =
0.104), economically large but statistically borderline given 34 games.
The restricted window (30--70') reduces the estimate to +1.36m,
revealing that parallel trends are imperfect. Honest conclusion: strong
descriptive tendency, causal identification fragile with this sample.

\begin{itemize}
\tightlist
\item
  \textbf{Section 5} - Is this behavior distinctive relative to elite
  Bundesliga teams, or standard pressing practice?
\end{itemize}

\emph{Answer:} Distinctively different. Leverkusen 2023/24 presses 5.5m
higher than the next-best team when losing late, has the highest press
maintenance index (+8.80m) in the benchmark, and sits in a quadrant no
2015/16 top team occupies. Most strikingly, Leverkusen 2015/16 shows the
exact opposite profile (-3.74m maintenance), confirming this is Alonso's
system not the club's identity.

\textbf{Treatment cell:} Losing \textbf{and} after minute 70 --- the
\emph{comeback mode}.

\textbf{Outcome:} \texttt{location.x}, the x-coordinate of each press
event (press height). Higher values = pressing further up the pitch =
more aggressive.

\textbf{DiD estimator:}
\[\hat{\beta}_{DiD} = \underbrace{(\bar{X}_{Losing,Late} - \bar{X}_{Losing,Early})}_{\text{change when losing}} - \underbrace{(\bar{X}_{NotLosing,Late} - \bar{X}_{NotLosing,Early})}_{\text{natural time trend}}\]

A potential concern is that the parallel trends assumption may not hold
in the full pre-treatment window, as the Losing group exhibits elevated
volatility in the opening 30 minutes --- consistent with acute tactical
reactions to early concessions rather than sustained pressing strategy.
To address this, we restrict the pre-treatment window to minutes 30--70,
where visual inspection confirms more stable parallel trends. Under this
specification, the DiD estimate falls to +1.36m (p = 0.739), suggesting
the full-window estimate partially reflects compositional differences in
the pre-treatment period. Given the limited sample of 34 matches, we
treat the full-window result as descriptive evidence of a comeback shift
tendency rather than a precise causal estimate.

Each panel shows a team's mean press height across the 4 DiD cells. The
key pattern to look for: does the Losing line (red) rise from Early to
Late? That is the comeback shift.

Leverkusen 2023/24 (bottom-left, bold lines): clear upward slope on the
Losing line (+2.5m early to late) while Not Losing drops sharply (-4m).
This creates the widening gap that defines comeback pressing.

Bayern: both lines are relatively flat and close together --- high
general pressing intensity but little situational adjustment.

Dortmund: Losing line rises notably from \textasciitilde60m to
\textasciitilde64m --- the 2015/16 team with the most similar profile to
Leverkusen 2023/24, though at a much lower absolute level.

Gladbach: lines converge late --- the gap between losing and not-losing
narrows after 70', suggesting they do not differentiate by score state.

Leverkusen 2015/16: most striking panel --- the Losing line DROPS from
early to late while Not Losing rises, producing a crossing pattern.

Under Xabi Alonso's predecessor, losing late meant pressing LOWER, the
complete tactical opposite of the 2023/24 system.
