# Pressing Under Pressure
### Spatial Analysis of Bayer Leverkusen's Comeback Pressing in the 2023/24 Bundesliga Season

> BSE Geospatial Data Science — Term Paper  
> March 2026

---

## Overview

Bayer Leverkusen's 2023/24 Bundesliga season was historically unprecedented — 34 matches unbeaten, built on a remarkable ability to recover from losing positions. This paper investigates whether that comeback identity has a measurable spatial signature in their pressing behavior.

Using StatsBomb event-level data, we combine **vector geometry**, **raster spatial analysis**, and a **Difference-in-Differences design** to answer three questions:

1. *Where* does Leverkusen spatially engineer turnovers through pressing traps?
2. *Does* Leverkusen shift their press forward when losing after the 70th minute — the "Comeback Shift"?
3. *Is* this behavior distinctive relative to elite Bundesliga teams, benchmarked against the 2015/16 season?

---

## Key Findings

- **Pressing traps** are concentrated in wide attacking channels, with the right channel (A3_R) achieving a 35.9% counterpress rate — robust even against top opponents
- **The Comeback Shift** — Leverkusen presses 6.2m higher when losing after 70' compared to the counterfactual trend (DiD estimate, p = 0.104). Economically large but statistically borderline given 34 matches
- **Benchmark comparison** — Leverkusen 2023/24 presses at 69.5m when losing late, 5.5m above the next-best team (Bayern Munich, 64.0m) across a benchmark of 2015/16 top-7 Bundesliga teams. Their press maintenance index (+8.80m) is the highest in the benchmark
- **Most striking finding** — Leverkusen 2015/16 shows the exact opposite profile (-3.74m press maintenance), confirming this is Alonso's tactical system, not a persistent club trait

## Methodological Notes

**DiD Design:**  
Treatment = Leverkusen losing AND after minute 70. Outcome = `location.x` (press height). The unit of observation is a pressing event, not a match — within-game variation in score state is exploited. Pre-treatment window restricted to minutes 30–70 in the robustness specification to satisfy the parallel trends assumption (early-game chaos violates comparability before minute 30).

**Parallel Trends:**  
Visual inspection confirms approximate parallel trends in the 30–70' window. The full window (0–70') estimate (+6.17m) is larger than the restricted window estimate (+1.36m), indicating partial sensitivity to pre-treatment window choice — acknowledged as a limitation given the 34-match sample.

**ELO Tiers:**  
Opponent quality is classified into tertiles (Top / Mid / Weak) based on ClubElo ratings of all 18 Bundesliga clubs on a pre-season anchor date (2023-08-18), ensuring thresholds do not shift as ratings update during the season.
